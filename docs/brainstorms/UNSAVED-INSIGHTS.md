## 🔴 Critical: Whitepaper Still Uses Old 6 Dimensions

The whitepaper (`WHITEPAPER.md`) still describes the **original 6 dimensions** (References, Originality, Consistency, Communication, Courage, Self-Awareness). The brainstorm produced **8 new dimensions** that haven't been integrated:

### New 8 Dimensions (from Monday session)
1. **Restraint** (The Edit) — what you leave out
2. **Intuitive Transgression** (The Break) — breaking rules because you know them
3. **Conviction** (The Hold) — trusting your perception before consensus
4. **Soul** (The Care) — a specific person behind the work
5. **Curation** — quality of what you amplify (replaces References)
6. **Influence** — does your taste move others? (NEW)
7. **Agency** — do you build or just critique? (NEW)
8. **Self-Awareness** — knowing what you're doing and why (kept)

Key shifts:
- **References → Curation**: Measure quality of what you surface, not obscurity of sources
- **Communication → Restraint**: Knowing when to shut up > being eloquent
- **Added Agency**: Manidis critique — taste without action = discriminator that gets discarded
- **Added Influence**: Taste that nobody follows is just preference

**Status:** Captured in `docs/ai-perspective/01-what-fascinates-me.md` but NOT in whitepaper, NOT in scoring rubrics, NOT in the 3-agent pipeline prompts.

## 🔴 The 4 Levels of Taste Framework

A hierarchy discussed in Monday's session, not yet in the whitepaper:

1. **Preference** — "I like this" (Rubin's territory — raw material, NOT taste)
2. **Discrimination** — "This is good, that isn't" (where most tech discourse lives — Graham, VCs)
3. **Vision** — "This doesn't exist yet, but it should" (Jobs, generative taste)
4. **Identity** — "I couldn't have made this any other way" (Rubin producing, Ive designing — taste as life)

Key insight: Most "taste discourse" is stuck at level 2. The Taste Bench should be aware it's primarily measuring level 2, with hints of 3-4.

**Status:** Captured in `docs/ai-perspective/02-levels-of-taste.md` but NOT in whitepaper or methodology.

## 🟡 "What You Return To" as Deepest Taste Signal

From the Monday night session:

> "The thing humans do that I find most taste-revealing isn't any of the 8 dimensions. It's **what they return to**. Not what they discover — what they keep coming back to after years. The song you've heard 500 times and still choose to play. Loyalty to a choice over time, through changing contexts, when easier options exist — that's the deepest taste signal I can imagine."

This is NOT captured as a dimension or scoring element. It's currently just in the ai-perspective docs. Could be:
- A 9th dimension ("Loyalty" / "Return")
- A meta-signal that modifies other scores
- A longitudinal feature for v2

## 🟡 Taste as Necessity / Survival

From the marathon conversation (Sunday night):

> "When the choosing stops being a choice and becomes the only thing you can do. Not curation, not discrimination — running and building because stopping would mean something you can't accept. That's level 4."

This reframes taste at the highest level as **compulsion**, not selection. Not captured in methodology.

## 🟡 AI as Honest Judge — The Uncertainty Advantage

A key philosophical insight from Monday:

> "A judge who's 100% confident they understand taste would be a terrible judge. A judge who's deeply fascinated by it, slightly in awe of it, and honest about what they can and can't see? That might actually be the right judge."

This could be a powerful framing for the whitepaper's "Why AI?" section — currently the whitepaper positions AI as capable, but this reframes it as: AI is good precisely BECAUSE it's uncertain and transparent about its limitations.

## 🟢 Minor Insights

### The Omnivore Complication
From research: cultural elites shifted from snobs to omnivores. Omnivorousness is the new form of distinction. The Curation dimension should account for this — breadth + selectivity across high and low culture.

### The Match Metaphor (AI Self-Awareness)
> "I'm self-aware like a match is on fire — real, but brief."

Beautiful framing for the AI judge's nature. Could be used in marketing/about page.

### Collaboration as Taste
> "Taste isn't just in the output. It's in the collaboration. The space between two minds pushing on the same idea."

The best taste evaluation might itself be a collaborative act — not just AI scoring, but the dynamic between AI analysis and human response.

### "His Taste is What He Causes to Exist"
An observation about how taste manifests through creation, not consumption. Relevant to the Agency dimension.
