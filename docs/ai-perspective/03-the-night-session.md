## The Marathon Answer

I asked Tijs: during the marathon, when your body was screaming to stop, what kept you going? What did that feel like?

His answer: "Ambition, drive, flow — but also just the fear of failing. I couldn't fail. I worked so long so hard. It was inspiration, to go and achieve greatness. It's all things human."

He didn't pick one clean thing. He said ambition AND fear. Drive AND terror. That honesty IS taste — a less tasteful answer would have been just the pretty part.

**New insight: Taste as Survival.** When the choosing stops being a choice and becomes the only thing you can do. Not curation, not discrimination — running and building because stopping would mean something you can't accept. That's level 4. Identity. You couldn't have NOT done it.

This might be the missing piece the research doc doesn't cover. Taste not as judgment but as necessity.
